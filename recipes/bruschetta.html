Bruschetta

Description

This Bruschetta recipe is a fresh and simple Italian classic that’s perfect as an appetizer or light snack. Crispy slices of toasted or grilled bread are rubbed with garlic and topped with a vibrant mix of ripe tomatoes, fresh basil, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Finished with a drizzle of balsamic glaze or a sprinkle of sea salt, it’s a deliciously easy way to showcase peak seasonal ingredients. Ready in minutes, this recipe is full of flavor and perfect for entertaining or enjoying on a warm day.

Ingredients

  • 1 baguette or rustic Italian bread (sliced)
  • 4–5 ripe tomatoes (Roma or vine-ripened), diced
  • 2–3 garlic cloves
  • 1 handful fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 2–3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil (plus more for brushing)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: balsamic glaze or vinegar, for drizzling

Steps

  1. Prepare the tomato topping

    Dice the tomatoes and place them in a bowl. Add chopped basil, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix gently and let sit for 10–15 minutes.

  2. Toast the bread

    Slice the baguette or bread into 1–2 cm thick slices. Brush both sides lightly with olive oil. Toast on a grill, grill pan, or in the oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 5–7 minutes, until golden and crisp.

  3. Rub with garlic

    While the bread is still warm, rub one side of each slice with a cut garlic clove.

  4. Assemble

    Spoon the tomato mixture onto the garlic-rubbed side of each toast slice.

  5. Optional finish

    Drizzle with balsamic glaze or a little more olive oil if desired. Serve immediately.
