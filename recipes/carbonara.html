Carbonara

Description

Indulge in the rich, creamy simplicity of a traditional Roman carbonara. This beloved pasta dish combines al dente spaghetti with crispy pancetta, tossed in a velvety sauce made from eggs, Parmesan, and black pepper—no cream, no shortcuts. The heat of the pasta gently cooks the egg mixture, creating a silky coating that clings to every strand. Perfectly balanced between salty, savory, and peppery, carbonara is Italian comfort food at its finest.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta (or guanciale), diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 cup (about 80g) grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta

    Boil a large pot of salted water and cook spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.

  2. Cook the pasta

    While pasta cooks, sauté pancetta in a large pan over medium heat until crispy. Turn off the heat.

  3. Cook the pasta

    In a bowl, whisk together eggs, yolk, cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.

  4. Cook the pasta

    Add hot drained pasta to the pan with pancetta. Pour in the egg mixture and toss quickly to coat, adding reserved pasta water a little at a time until the sauce is silky and smooth.

  5. Cook the pasta

    Plate immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.
