Chilli

Description

Hearty, bold, and packed with flavor, this Classic American Chili is the ultimate comfort food. Made with tender ground beef, slow-simmered tomatoes, kidney beans, and a rich blend of spices like cumin, paprika, and chili powder, it's the perfect balance of smoky heat and savory depth. Whether you're feeding a crowd or meal-prepping for the week, this one-pot wonder delivers cozy satisfaction in every spoonful. Top it with shredded cheese, sour cream, and green onions for the full experience. Great on its own or ladled over fries, rice, or nachos — this chili is an American classic done right.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 400g (14 oz) canned diced tomatoes
  • 400g (14 oz) canned kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 400g (14 oz) canned black beans, drained and rinsed (optional but adds texture)
  • 1 cup beef stock or water
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for extra heat)
  • 1½ tsp salt, or to taste
  • ½ tsp black pepper, or to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (to balance acidity)

Steps

  1. Heat The Oil

    In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium heat.

  2. Sauté the aromatics

    Add diced onion and cook for 3–4 minutes until softened. Stir in minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Brown the beef

    Add the ground beef, breaking it apart with a spoon. Cook until browned (about 5–7 minutes). Drain excess fat if necessary.

  4. Add bell pepper and tomato paste

    Stir in diced red bell pepper and tomato paste. Cook for 2–3 minutes to slightly caramelize the paste and soften the pepper.

  5. Add spices

    Sprinkle in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, cayenne (optional), salt, pepper, and sugar. Stir to evenly coat the beef and vegetables in the spices.

  6. Add tomatoes, beans, and stock

    Pour in the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, black beans (if using), and beef stock. Stir to combine everything.

  7. Simmer

    Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover partially and simmer for 30–45 minutes, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the richer the flavor.

  8. Adjust seasoning

    Taste and adjust salt, pepper, or spices as needed. If it's too thick, add a splash of water or stock. If too thin, simmer uncovered a little longer.

  9. Serve and top

    Ladle into bowls and top with your favorite toppings: cheddar cheese, sour cream, green onions, jalapeños, or corn chips.
